Code of Conduct_

This code of conduct was developed on March 21st 2024. We recognise that these terms may require revision and further amendments, which may be changed at a later date.

The code of conduct is split into three sections, each covering specific aspects of the A.S.T.R.A.L. Institute. The first, called "Vision", highlights the basic principles and ethos of A.S.T.R.A.L, from which the other aspects of the code of conduct stem from. The second section, titled "Work", outlines work-related practices that A.S.T.R.A.L. members strive to uphold and aspire to. This section in particular notes the expectations and responsibilities of students and mentors in work-related environments. Finally, the third section, titled "Relationships", draws attention to the relationships between members and the relationships between mentors and parents of students to ensure a safe and positive environment for all. Below, you can go to any section, or simply continue scrolling to view the full code of conduct.

Vision Work Relationships

Vision

  • Code of conduct applies on and off campus, and on all electronic platforms used such as zoom/teams. All actively involved members of the A.S.T.R.A.L. Institute's programs (students and mentors) should respect and adhere to the code of conduct.
  • Providing opportunities for all students to contribute to work, regardless of skills or experience
  • Welcoming workplace for people from a wide range of backgrounds. This includes neurodiversity, gender diversity, and students from culturally diverse or diverse socioeconomic backgrounds.
  • An environment that promotes curiosity
    • The ability for students to ask questions freely
    • The ability to encourage students to explore, and develop themselves
  • Working in parallel with the UN Sustainable Goals 2050.
  • Create opportunities for networking and collaboration beyond the program, connecting participants with mentors and experts in their field.

Work

  • Clear ownership of projects and acknowledgement of contributions
  • Adapting to a student's circumstances and other commitments, such as studies, and thus not creating extra stress through extra workloads.
  • Constructive criticisms
    • Respecting other's work, ideas, contributions and effort
    • Clear and respectful ways/opportunities to give feedback
    • Able to receive and take time to reflect upon criticism without an automatic negative response
  • Awareness of team first results over individuals
  • Clear goals set which are S.M.A.R.T. (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, timely) for all, and is adapted to everyone's circumstances.
  • Maintaining data integrity and transparency of scientific processes. This is achieved through accurate documentation of such processes.
  • A space where people are encouraged to meet their goals based on prior agreements and in reference to the code of conduct. This includes arriving and departing to events on time, and fulfilling time commitments
  • Dedicated to teaching/learning new skills without judgement, regardless of previous knowledge, both between Astralnauts and from mentors

Relationships

  • Reassurance for parents about student safety:
    • Mentors have a Working With Children Check (WWCC)
    • Students generally mentored in teams, not one-on-one
    • Parents informed of any off-campus social activities
    • 6-monthly catch up for parents or guardians of school children.
    • Appointment of Ombuds
    • A safe space where students can share any concerns they might have with their mentors.
    • Respectful social distance between mentors and school children. This includes school students referring mentors by appropriate titles. An example is Dr. Flynn, not Chris.
  • Self-awareness so everyone can contribute
    • Contributions from all team members valued and celebrated
    • People should be able to speak freely with their ideas and/or questions without belittlement. Their contributions should be welcomed
    • If people need help they should feel that they can reach out and ask
  • Resolve issues that can lead to gossip before it spreads. Address issues with respect and dignity and in a positive manner
    • Any potential issues are resolved through a methodological, pre-determined process (this to be decided)
    • Having clear communication lines between students, mentors & parents/guardians
  • Respectful environment where nobody should be bullied because of the background, gender, creed, ethnicity, physical appearance, etc. This also includes comments or remarks that are perceived as bullying or harassment should not be explained away as “just a joke” (casual remarks can be hurtful).
